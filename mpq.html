Mortgage Pre-Qualification Tool: Bridging the Gap to Home Ownership

A lead generation tool giving prospective home buyers a mortgage amount estimate in exchange for basic inputs — connecting them with a Coast mortgage specialist. Built from scratch in a team of 1 PM, 2 engineers, and 1 designer.

Background

Coast Capital wanted to provide a way for prospective home buyers to explore their borrowing capacity and mortgage options before committing to a mortgage pre-approval. Our team was tasked with creating a new tool from scratch that would serve as a bridge to generate leads for the mortgage team to connect with prospective home buyers. I want to share the story of how I designed this experience and some reflections on the challenges we faced.

Problem

We first had to identify who we were designing our tool for and develop an educated guess on what kind of tool our users would expect. We were expected to market the tool to both Coast members and non-members alike. For non-members, this meant that we had to provide a competitive experience similar enough to competitors. For members, this meant we had to provide familiarity and brand recognition that Coast members would recognize and trust. The business wanted to take a frontal strategy and release a product with similar strengths that could compete directly with competitors. The Mortgage Specialist team that would be receiving these leads also wanted to provide a service that conveyed their goal to "under promise, over deliver".

Process

We started by examining competitor products under the assumption that prospective homebuyers would be comparing pre-qualifications from multiple institutions to determine their available mortgage options. We completed rounds of iterations based first on our understanding of our members' needs, then validated those assumptions using stakeholder feedback, internal staff, and eventually real users.

Design principles

Simple Minimise user input where possible to deliver results quickly and get users connected to mortgage specialists faster.
Educative Provide value by presenting relevant information for prospective home buyers when they need it.

We used the feedback to bring our tool closer to what prospective homebuyers were expecting, as well as identified areas of improvement that were beyond scope. Some key tasks we identified through testing included:

  • Refine landing page language to establish the new tool's purpose
  • Simplify the form layout of their results to allow users to scan more effectively
  • Provide more guidance and copy during the identity verification step

Challenges

Initially, one of our key stakeholders was dead set on building the exact same tool as one of our competitors. With continuous involvement throughout the testing process, we were able to inspire confidence and buy-in for our proposed designs by testing our key stakeholders' assumptions.

Midway through the design phase, we underwent a company-wide brand refresh. This provided many challenges to adapt our existing designs to the new brand guidelines. As a result, I worked very closely with our Lead Visual Designer to develop components that passed guidelines as well as followed the constraints of Pixel, our newly developed design system.

We also faced the challenge of not owning the web page that led into our tool. While we were confident our team had the knowledge and data to understand what would bring home owners to our tool, final ownership was kept with another team. I communicated what we had learned from communicating with home owners, ultimately it was challenging collaborating with a team that firmly stuck to their own vision.

Outcomes

Due to additional requirements added to the business strategy, product launch was delayed until July 2022. Since then, our tool has been used to connect thousands of Canadians with Coast Capital Mortgage Specialists and continues to serve as an engaging first touchpoint for prospective home owners at Coast Capital.

Many of the components that were introduced as a result of this project have been adopted, iterated, and implemented into the core banking project. Additionally, the UI patterns established in this project served as the starting point for Coast Capital's current form experience.
